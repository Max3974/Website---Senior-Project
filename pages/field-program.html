Create Field Program

A Field Program constitutes any research you've done in the field during this reporting period. This may be a cruise, it may be land-based research. Regardless, it all goes here.

  • Cruise Name: While not every cruise is officially named, it's important to give that cruise/program a name that conforms to cruise identifications. Be sure to include the name of the vessel if applicable.
  • Expedition: The tag associated with your expedition (ex. EX1103 Leg 1).
  • NOAA Related: check if NOAA was involved in any way (other than NOAA OCS being the Center's primary funding agent). This includes any NOAA personnel working with you, visiting you, or doing this for or with NOAA division or group.
  • Tagged CCOM Members: enter any personnel here who may have sailed/participated in the program with you. As you type, a drop-down menu will automatically populate (when you stop typing). This is not a required field.
  • Date: Enter the date. If the event lasted more than one day, check the box labeled "Show End Date" and second Date field will appear.
  • Summary: Write a general description here in two or three sentences - IN THE THIRD PERSON, PLEASE. Keep this section brief. If there are specifics, or broader topics to discuss where this event is concerned, please put that info in the Narrative section of your report.

Keep in mind that this section is merely a listing and brief description of your role in the Field Program; if there are broader topics to discuss where this is concerned, it's best to include this in the narrative, particularly if it's relevant to your core research.
